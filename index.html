Background

Hi, I’m Pranav Pramod, a computer science student at the Morris County School of Technology and a member of the Class of 2027. I’ve been building my skills as a developer for around six years, and over time, I’ve become especially interested in data science, software engineering, and using technology to solve meaningful problems.

Purpose

I’m currently based in Denville, New Jersey, and I’m interning at Talon Biomarkers, where I support cell analysis through data analytics in flow cytometry. I’m drawn to projects where code has a real purpose, whether that means improving accessibility, expanding education, or helping people interact with technology in a simpler way.

Collaboration

A lot of my best work has come from collaboration. I believe that when people bring different ideas and skills together, the final result is usually stronger, more creative, and more useful than anything one person could build alone.

Sophomore Year

Over the past few years, I’ve competed in a variety of technical competitions that have pushed me to grow as both a developer and a teammate. In my sophomore year, my team and I became national finalists in the NASA App Development Challenge after building a simulation of NASA’s Artemis II mission and analyzing mission data. That summer, we competed in EmpowerHacks 2.0, where we created an educational platform inspired by the UN’s fourth Sustainable Development Goal: quality education. Our goal was to make STEM learning more accessible to underrepresented communities, and we ended up as one of the winning teams.

Junior Year

In my junior year, I competed at TSA States in Software Development and Engineering Design. For Software Development, my team built an app to help visually impaired users order food more easily, placing fourth overall. For Engineering Design, we created a theoretical solution for detecting life on Europa, one of Jupiter’s moons, using microfluidic technology. We placed first and will be competing at TSA Nationals in Washington, D.C. I also competed in Garden State CTF, where my team placed and was invited to the Garden State Cyber Summit. There, we participated as blue team specialists in a live-fire exercise focused on mitigating a ransomware attack. We placed fourth, and it was one of the most fun and intense cybersecurity experiences I’ve had so far.
